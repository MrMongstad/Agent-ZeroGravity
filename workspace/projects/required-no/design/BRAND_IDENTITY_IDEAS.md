# Required.no — Brand Identity & Naming Exploration

> **Status:** 🔒 LOCKED (Hybrid Approach)
> **Created:** 2026-04-19
> **Focus:** Playing with phonetics, the letter 'Q', and visual identity for "Required".

---

## 🔒 Locked Decision: The Brutalist Capital Q

We are moving forward with a hybrid approach that maximizes the **"Humanize"** protocol:
- **Structure (From Direction 4):** Standard spelling (`reQuired.no`) for SEO safety, but with an oversized, baseline-breaking **Q** to create organic asymmetry.
- **Palette & Texture (From Direction 2):** We strip out neon tech colors. The palette is heavy, Nordic, and physical: Charcoal Black, Crisp White, Paper-Grain texture, and industrial accent colors (like Rust Red or Caution Yellow).

---

## 1. The "ReQwired" Direction (Rewired / Required)

![ReQwired Logo](file:///c:/Users/steph/Desktop/Antigravity%20and%20Agent%200/workspace/projects/required-no/design/logos/01_reQwired.jpg)

**Concept:** Playing on the word "Rewired" while keeping the phonetic sound of "Required". 
**Visuals:** `ReQwired` or `re:Qwired`

**Why it works:**
- It implies **transformation**. You aren't just building things; you are *rewiring* how digital experiences work.
- It feels highly technical, energetic, and slightly "hacker-ethos".
- The "W" adds a sharp, architectural angle to the logo.

**Logo Idea:** 
- The tail of the **Q** loops out and becomes a physical wire that connects to the **dot** in `.no`. 
- High-tech, clean monospaced font where the 'Q' is the hero element.

---

## 2. The "ReQierd" Direction (Phonetic / Nordic)

**Concept:** A phonetic, shorthand spelling. 
**Visuals:** `ReQierd` or `rQierd`

**Why it works:**
- Removing the "u" creates a slightly awkward but memorable visual pause. 
- It feels vaguely Nordic or European minimalist. 
- It forces the reader to look twice, which increases brand retention.

**Logo Idea:** 
- Very brutalist typography. Thick, heavy lettering with tight kerning. 
- The uppercase **Q** sits in the middle like a monolithic object.

---

## 3. The "Vowel-Drop" Direction (rQrd)

![RQRD Branding Iron](file:///c:/Users/steph/Desktop/Antigravity%20and%20Agent%200/workspace/projects/required-no/design/logos/03_RQRD_Vowel_Drop.jpg)

**Concept:** Stripping the word down to its absolute bare bones.
**Visuals:** `rQrd.no` or `RQRD`

**Why it works:**
- Capitalizes on the minimalist tech trend (like Stripe, Plaid, Vercel). 
- Looks incredibly sleek on a hoodie or hardware.
- Fast to type, visually symmetrical.

**Logo Idea:** 
- A geometric logo where the letters `R Q R D` form a perfect square or grid. 
- Ultimate "Humanize" angle: It looks almost like a stamped physical seal or branding iron.

---

## 4. The "Capital Q" Anchor (reQuired)

![Capital Q Anchor](file:///c:/Users/steph/Desktop/Antigravity%20and%20Agent%200/workspace/projects/required-no/design/logos/04_reQuired_Capital_Q.png)

**Concept:** Keep the correct spelling for SEO and clarity, but manipulate the capitalization and styling to create the logo mark.
**Visuals:** `reQuired` or `[re]Quired`

**Why it works:**
- No confusion on how to spell the domain name, but still visually distinct.
- The uppercase Q breaks the baseline of the letters, creating organic asymmetry (fitting the new Humanize protocol).

**Logo Idea:** 
- The word is written in lowercase, except for a massively oversized, stylized **Q**.
- The `re` acts as a prefix (like "reply" or "regarding"). It suggests iteration: doing it again, doing it better.

## 5. The "re:Q" Abbreviation

![re:Q App Icon](file:///c:/Users/steph/Desktop/Antigravity%20and%20Agent%200/workspace/projects/required-no/design/logos/05_reQ_Abbreviation.png)

**Concept:** The full name is Required, but the logo and visual shorthand is just `re:Q`.
**Visuals:** `re:Q`

**Why it works:**
- "re: Q" implies a response to a question or a query. You are the answer. 
- Highly versatile for social media avatars, favicons, and watermarks.
